## Abstract

This comprehensive study examines the relationship between solar positioning, ambient temperature, and napping quality over a 7-year observation period. Our findings indicate a strong correlation between sunbeam intensity and sleep depth, with optimal conditions occurring between 2:00 PM and 4:00 PM in south-facing windows.

### Key Findings

1. **Window orientation matters**: South-facing windows provide 47% more usable sunbeam hours than east or west orientations.

2. **Surface material**: Wooden floors retain heat better than carpet, but carpet provides superior cushioning. The ideal solution: a blanket on a wooden floor.

3. **Interrupted sunbeams**: Clouds reduce nap quality by 34%. Humans walking through sunbeams cause a 78% quality decrease.

## Discussion

These results have profound implications for feline welfare. Households should consider:

- Installing larger south-facing windows
- Removing all obstacles between windows and napping surfaces
- Training humans to avoid sunbeam disruption
